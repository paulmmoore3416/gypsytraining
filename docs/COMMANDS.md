## ðŸ“‹ BASIC OBEDIENCE

### SIT
**Verbal:** "Sit"
**Hand Signal:** Flat hand, palm up, raised from waist to chest
**When to Use:** Before doors, meals, greetings, stops on walks

### DOWN / LAY DOWN
**Verbal:** "Down"
**Hand Signal:** Flat hand pointing to ground, sweeping downward
**When to Use:** Restaurants, waiting rooms, long stays, calming

### STAY
**Verbal:** "Stay"
**Hand Signal:** Open palm facing dog, held steady
**When to Use:** Safety, distance work, impulse control

### COME / HERE
**Verbal:** "Come"
**Hand Signal:** Arm extended, sweeping toward body
**When to Use:** Emergency recall, check-in, return to handler

### HEEL
**Verbal:** "Heel"
**Hand Signal:** Pat left thigh
**When to Use:** Public access, crowd navigation, close control

### PLACE / MAT
**Verbal:** "Place"
**Hand Signal:** Point to designated spot
**When to Use:** Settle in public, boundary training

### LEAVE IT
**Verbal:** "Leave it"
**Hand Signal:** Closed fist shown to dog
**When to Use:** Food on ground, trash, distractions

### WAIT
**Verbal:** "Wait"
**Hand Signal:** Open palm (temporary hold)
**When to Use:** Doorways, thresholds, before release

### OFF
**Verbal:** "Off"
**Hand Signal:** Push away motion
**When to Use:** Get off furniture/person, prevent jumping

### RELEASE
**Verbal:** "Free" / "Okay" / "Release"
**Hand Signal:** Sweeping release gesture
**When to Use:** End of any position hold

## âœ… TRAINING SUCCESS TIPS

1. **ONE COMMAND RULE:** Say command once. Repeating = teaching her to wait.

2. **TIMING IS EVERYTHING:** Reward within 1-2 seconds of correct behavior.

3. **CONSISTENCY WINS:** Same word, same signal, every single time.

4. **TONE MATTERS:** Calm and confident > loud and frustrated.

5. **MARKER WORD:** "Yes!" marks exact moment of success.

6. **HIGH-VALUE REWARDS:** Chicken, cheese, hot dogs for difficult tasks.

7. **PROOF IN MANY PLACES:** Practice everywhere, not just at home.

8. **RELEASE REQUIRED:** Every position needs a release cue.

9. **SHORT SESSIONS:** 10-15 focused minutes > 60 distracted minutes.

10. **END ON SUCCESS:** Always finish with something she does well.
